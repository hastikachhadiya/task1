Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015


Missile Man of India | Bharat Ratna | Former President of India

"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping;
Dream is something that does not let you sleep."


  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1955: Joined Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and worked on SLV-3 Satellite Launch
  • 1998: Played key role in Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests
  • 2002–2007: President of India
  • 2015: Passed away while giving a lecture — died inspiring students

Achievements

Dr. Kalam received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan. He authored many inspirational books such as Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds. His contributions in missile technology made India a recognized power globally.


Biography

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the greatest scientists and leaders the country has ever produced. He played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. Kalam also served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

He was admired for his humility, wisdom, and dedication to inspiring young minds. His life is a perfect example of how dreams, hard work, and determination can lift a simple boy from Rameswaram to the highest office in the nation.
